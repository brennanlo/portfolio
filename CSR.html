CSR Templates — Streamlining insurance claim processes

Company / WSIB Innovation Lab
Design / Brennan Lo (me) & Christina Ru
Development / Jeff Talbot
Tools / Sketch & Invision
Timeline / 3 Weeks

Overview

The Kitchener WSIB call center receives hundreds of calls daily. From those calls, customer service representatives (CSRs) receive them, and help callers file insurance claims. Currently, the system of processing insurance claims requires the completion of multiple templates, which is complicated, difficult to use, and unintuitive.

Problem

Due to the nature of the current insurance claim process, customer service representatives and management are frustrated with the template system. In its current state, there are far too many templates to work with and are very difficult to use and choose.

From left: Insurance claim touch points flow chart, No-lost-time triage template.

Goal

Streamline the insurance claim process by unifying the templates into a singular form that acts as a single source of truth.

Research

The design team collectively job shadowed and interviewed customer service representatives, eligibility adjudicators, case managers, and return-to-work specialists to gain a better understanding of the problem with the existing solution.

Customer Service Representatives

  • Those new to the template system were unsure of which templates to use for each claim type
  • Callers are left on long hold times to search for appropriate documents to fill out templates
  • Many redundant questions and scripts appear in the templates
  • Current UI is old and disorganized

Eligibility Adjudicators

  • Templates are difficult to read, and scraping relevant information from them are difficult

Case Managers

  • Templates indicate important information poorly

Empathize

From the information that was collected through our shadowing and interviews, we synthesized user personas and user journey maps to aid in creating a solution that met the needs and goals of our users .

USER PERSONAS

I created 2 personas for an experienced and inexperienced CSR. I also made sure to feature two different layouts for the personas. One was used to easily compare both personas side by side at a glance, and the other provided all the user information in one setting.

USER JOURNEYS

Two types of user journeys were made. One was in the perspective of the caller making a claim, and the other was of a customer service representative.

POC 1 — Guided Form Picker

The design team decided that a good first step towards simplifying the template system would be to create a guided form picker. This would help introduce newer CSRs to the template system, and help guide them towards picking the correct template. Invision was used in order to create a prototype that could be easily used for usability testing later on.

During this POC, our main goals were to create a simple UI that would be easy for CSRs to navigate.

The idea of this prototype was to have the form auto-populate itself as you move along selecting radio buttons and dropdowns. This allows for decreased clutter and confusion for CSRs.

Once CSRs reach the workplan activity, they have to run through a list of exceptions to determine the next steps to be taken. Showcasing the exceptions in this format ensures that CSRs review each exception case before moving forward. This reduces the amount of mistakes that could possibly be made and ensures that claims don't flow down the wrong path because of a missed exception.

POC 2

The second POC was used to merge the ESU and triage templates together. This was a step towards the goal of having one singular form as a single souce of truth instead of having multiple templates.

Since these two templates already had content that was similar within it, merging these templates would act as a good starting point. This would eliminate redundancies between using the two tempaltes and create a good starting point to merge the rest of the templates later on.

Next Steps

User testing has yet to be done on the new ESU/Triage form. Feedback from the corporate CSR manager has also not been given as of yet. As the new design team for the future term takes over this project, they will determine the scope of the next POC.
